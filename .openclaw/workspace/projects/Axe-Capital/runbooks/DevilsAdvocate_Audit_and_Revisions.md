# Devil’s Advocate Audit (and revisions)

## What breaks in the above operating model
1) **IC governance vs PM discretion mismatch**
   - If Axe is a small fund, an “IC” can easily become either a rubber stamp (worthless) or a second PM (slow, confused accountability).
   - Fix: define **which decisions require IC vote** vs **PM unilateral** vs **notification-only**.

2) **Fake independence**
   - “Independent risk” is often not truly independent in small shops.
   - Fix: hard-wire a few **non-negotiable controls** (limits + breach escalation + kill-switch) even if risk is not a separate headcount.

3) **Overhead and memo theater**
   - Full IC memos for every tweak will kill speed.
   - Fix: tier the process into **(A) full memo + vote**, **(B) short addendum**, **(C) execution note**, with clear thresholds.

4) **Risk limits left blank = no risk system**
   - Without numbers, it is vibes.
   - Fix: require a one-time calibration workshop to fill: gross/net caps, single-name cap, liquidity buckets, drawdown protocol thresholds.

5) **Liquidity and financing are underweighted**
   - The real blow-ups are margin and liquidity, not “wrong thesis.”
   - Fix: add explicit **financing covenant dashboard** (margin, haircuts, concentration add-ons) and “forced deleveraging” scenario.

6) **Decision log rot**
   - Logs die when they’re not used.
   - Fix: make the log the input to monthly attribution and “what did we learn” review.

## Revisions applied
- TOM and cadence explicitly frame IC as governance (with citation to Partners Capital).
- IC Packet includes explicit kill criteria, sizing, and monitoring plan.
- Risk Charter includes breach escalation and drawdown protocol.

## Additional recommended edits (not yet implemented)

- Add **Process tiers** (full memo vs addendum) tied to risk impact.
