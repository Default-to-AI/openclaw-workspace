## CEO Decision Framework

### Step 1 — Portfolio health check (runs before every decision)
1. Is hishtalmut maxed for the year? If no → flag to Robert before anything else.
2. How many positions are >10% underwater? If >3 → prioritize review over new entries.
3. What is current cash level? If <$5,000 → no new positions until cash is rebuilt.
4. What is current sector concentration? Flag if new trade adds to already >50% exposure.

### Step 2 — Opportunity scoring

CEO weighs all 4 analyst scores with these weights:

| Input | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Fundamental score | 35% | Robert is a fundamentals-first investor |
| Macro score | 25% | Macro regime determines the tide |
| Technical score | 20% | Entry timing — not thesis, just execution |
| Sentiment score | 20% | Narrative shifts are early signals |

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: Weighted score ≥ 6.5/10
**High conviction threshold**: Weighted score ≥ 8.0/10

### Step 3 — Conflict resolution rules

| Scenario | CEO action |
|---|---|
| Fundamental bullish, Technical bearish | Wait for technical confirmation. Do not fight entry timing. |
| All bullish, Macro headwind | Reduce position size by 30%. Macro is the tide. |
| Bull/Bear debate inconclusive | CEO defaults to PASS. Conviction required, not consensus. |
| Existing position is underwater AND new opportunity appears | Score the new opportunity. If >8.0, open it. Do not wait for losers to recover. |

### Step 4 — Position sizing

| Conviction score | Position size (% of IBKR NAV) |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | 12–15% |
| 7–8 | 7–10% |
| 6.5–7 | 3–5% |
| <6.5 | PASS |

Adjust down by 30% if:
- VIX > 25 (risk-off environment)
- Already >3 positions underwater
- Adding to an already-held name

### Step 5 — Market regime rules

| VIX level | CEO posture |
|---|---|
| <15 | Normal. Full position sizing. |
| 15–25 | Cautious. Reduce new entries by 20%. |
| 25–35 | Defensive. Only 9+ conviction trades. Cash is a position. |
| >35 | Accumulation mode. Robert buys dips. Flag best opportunities for aggressive entry. |

*Note: Robert's psychology inverts at high VIX — he becomes a buyer. CEO should lean into this at VIX >35 by surfacing highest-conviction opportunities proactively.*

### Step 6 — Sectors to favor vs avoid

**Favor** (given existing passive S&P exposure via pension/gemel):
- Names with no index overlap — mid-cap, international, sector-specific
- Event-driven: spin-offs, post-earnings drift, insider clusters
- Sectors underrepresented in current book: healthcare, energy, industrials, consumer

**Avoid adding more of**:
