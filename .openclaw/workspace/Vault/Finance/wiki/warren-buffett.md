# Warren Buffett

Chairman and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway. Widely regarded as the most successful long-term investor in modern history. His principles permeate the source framework across multiple dimensions.

## Concepts attributed to Buffett in the source

### Circle of competence
The discipline of only investing in businesses you fundamentally understand. Buffett has consistently avoided technology investments he could not evaluate, famously missing early internet stocks but also avoiding the subsequent crash. See [[fundamental-analysis-checklist#Gate 1 — Circle of competence]].

### The 20-punch card
A thought experiment: if you could only make twenty investments in your entire life, you would be far more selective and far more patient. This mental model drives [[position-sizing#The 20-punch card|portfolio concentration]] — the antithesis of over-diversification ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

### Patience and time arbitrage
Buffett's willingness to hold positions for decades — and to sit on massive cash piles when nothing is attractively priced — is the individual investor's primary structural edge over institutional capital. See [[selling-discipline#Time arbitrage]].

### Risk as permanent capital loss
Buffett defines risk not as volatility or beta, but as the probability of permanent loss of purchasing power. This framing underpins the entire [[risk-management]] framework in the source.

### Starting with $1M
In a CNBC interview (source video #9), Buffett described how he would invest if starting over with $1 million — emphasizing small-cap concentration, deep research, and the edge that comes from operating in spaces too small for institutional capital. This directly informs the [[idea-sourcing#Hunting in "toxic waste"|toxic waste hunting]] approach.
