# Idea Sourcing

The process of generating a pipeline of potential investment candidates before any deep analysis begins. In hedge-fund-style stock picking, the quality and differentiation of your idea pipeline determines your ceiling for alpha generation.

## Hunting in "toxic waste"

The highest-returning opportunities cluster where institutional capital structurally cannot or will not participate. This includes:

- **Spin-offs** — newly independent companies often dumped by index funds that held the parent. Dense legal filings deter casual analysis, creating informational edges for those willing to read them.
- **Bankruptcies and restructurings** — businesses emerging from Chapter 11 or undergoing complex corporate surgery. Wall Street coverage typically evaporates for years, leaving these companies in an analytical vacuum.
- **Micro/small caps with no analyst coverage** — companies too small for institutional mandates. The fewer eyes on a stock, the wider the gap between price and intrinsic value.

This principle traces back to [[joel-greenblatt]]'s work and is echoed repeatedly in [[jack-schwager|Schwager's]] Market Wizards interviews ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

## Quantitative screening

Automated screens compress thousands of stocks into a workable list. The source identifies several high-signal metrics:

| Metric | Signal |
|--------|--------|
| High ROIC + High Earnings Yield | Core Greenblatt "magic formula" combination |
| Low EV/EBITDA | Enterprise value cheapness relative to cash earnings |
| Strong Price-to-Free-Cash-Flow | Actual cash generation vs. market price |
| High cap rate vs. bond yields | Real-asset yield comparison (relevant for asset plays) |

Screens are a *filter*, not a decision. They generate candidates; [[fundamental-analysis-checklist|fundamental analysis]] provides the conviction.

## The local edge

Coined by [[peter-lynch|Peter Lynch]], the local edge leverages everyday observation as an information source that precedes Wall Street modeling:

- Products visibly flying off shelves at retail locations
- Industry trends observable within your own profession
- Personal spending pattern shifts (what are you and your peers buying more/less of?)

The insight is that consumers and industry insiders encounter demand signals months before they appear in quarterly earnings reports ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

## Crisis and tax-loss opportunities

Two recurring windows of systemic mispricing:

1. **Year-end tax-loss selling** — investors dump losing positions in Q4 for tax harvesting, creating artificial selling pressure on already-beaten-down stocks. These stocks frequently rebound in January once the forced selling ceases. This is a core strategy of contrarian investors like Benj Gallander ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

2. **Macroeconomic panics** — recessions, credit crises, and geopolitical shocks trigger indiscriminate selling. Fundamentally sound businesses get dragged down alongside genuinely impaired ones. Having [[selling-discipline#Holding cash|dry powder]] during these moments is what separates opportunistic investors from victims.
