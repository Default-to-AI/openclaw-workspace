# Hedge Fund Strategy Research — Source Summary

Synthesized from a 9-video YouTube research batch processed through NotebookLM on 2026-04-11. The source channels include [[jack-schwager|Jack Schwager]]-adjacent content, [[warren-buffett|Warren Buffett]] advice compilations, [[peter-lynch|Peter Lynch]] methodology breakdowns, contrarian investing interviews, and recession-opportunity analyses.

## Core thesis

Individual investors can achieve market-beating returns by combining hedge-fund-style analytical rigor with their one structural advantage over institutions: patience and a long time horizon. The framework spans the full investment lifecycle from idea generation through exit.

## Seven-pillar framework

### 1. Idea sourcing and screening

Opportunities concentrate where institutions refuse to look — spin-offs, bankruptcies, restructurings, and thinly covered small caps. Quantitative screens (high ROIC + high earnings yield, low EV/EBITDA, strong P/FCF) narrow the universe. The "local edge" — noticing real-world consumer trends before analysts model them — supplements systematic screening. Tax-loss selling windows and macro panics create additional entry points. See [[idea-sourcing]].

### 2. Fundamental analysis checklist

Four gates: (a) circle of competence — discard what you cannot explain; (b) balance sheet health — zero or declining long-term debt; (c) management alignment — high insider ownership, open-market buying, reasonable compensation; (d) red flags — inventory growing faster than sales, reckless M&A, reverse splits, unsustainably high dividend yields (>7%). See [[fundamental-analysis-checklist]].

### 3. Variant perception and catalysts

Alpha comes from identifying where the market's pricing model is structurally wrong. Three patterns: bimodal outcomes mispriced as bell curves, free optionality on ignored assets/catalysts, and thesis-first-but-price-confirms entry timing. See [[variant-perception]].

### 4. Position sizing and concentration

Asymmetric payoff structures are non-negotiable. The [[kelly-criterion|Half-Kelly strategy]] sizes positions at half the mathematically optimal bet to capture most of the upside while slashing volatility. Buffett's "20-punch card" mental model enforces extreme selectivity. True diversification means uncorrelated exposures across business life cycles (slow growers, stalwarts, fast growers, cyclicals, turnarounds, asset plays). See [[position-sizing]].

### 5. Risk management

Pre-define invalidation points before entry. The 3% rule: halve a position at -3%, liquidate on further decline. Replace hard stop-losses with mental stops and incremental liquidation to manage psychology. Time-based stops free capital from stagnant ideas. Risk is permanent capital loss, not volatility. See [[risk-management]].

### 6. Selling discipline

Time arbitrage — holding through multi-year drawdowns that force institutional sellers out — is the individual investor's primary edge. Cash is a strategic weapon, not idle capital. Set sell targets before entry; harvest incrementally on the way up; exit fully when the thesis breaks. See [[selling-discipline]].

### 7. Behavioral traps

Four lethal patterns: ego-driven loss aversion (holding losers to avoid admitting error, selling winners to lock in validation), leverage converting temporary drawdowns into permanent losses, confusing profitable outcomes with sound process, and herd-following (tips, momentum chasing, buying at peak euphoria). See [[behavioral-traps]].
