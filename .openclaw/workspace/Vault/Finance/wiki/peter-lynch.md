# Peter Lynch

Former manager of the Fidelity Magellan Fund (1977-1990), which averaged 29.2% annual returns under his tenure — one of the best track records in mutual fund history. His investing philosophy is heavily represented in the source framework, particularly through the "We Study Billionaires" episode featuring Kyle Grieve's analysis of *One Up on Wall Street*.

## Concepts attributed to Lynch in the source

### The local edge
Lynch's most distinctive contribution: ordinary investors encounter investment opportunities in their daily lives before Wall Street analysts model them. Noticing which products are selling, which stores are packed, which services your friends are adopting — these observations translate into investable insights months before they appear in quarterly earnings. See [[idea-sourcing#The local edge]] ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

### Six stock categories
Lynch classified stocks into six categories, each with different risk/return profiles and roles in a portfolio:

1. **Slow growers** — large, mature companies with low growth but stable dividends
2. **Stalwarts** — large caps growing at 10-12% annually; reliable core holdings
3. **Fast growers** — small to mid-cap companies growing 20-50%+; primary return drivers
4. **Cyclicals** — companies whose earnings fluctuate with the economic cycle
5. **Turnarounds** — distressed companies with recovery potential
6. **Asset plays** — companies whose balance sheets contain hidden value

This classification system directly informs the [[position-sizing#Strategic diversification|strategic diversification]] framework: genuine diversification means holding exposures across multiple categories, not just multiple stocks within the same category.
