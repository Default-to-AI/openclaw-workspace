# Jack D. Schwager

Author of the *Market Wizards* book series — a collection of interviews with top traders and hedge fund managers that has become a canonical reference in trading and investing education. The source framework draws on a summary of *Hedge Fund Market Wizards* (source video #2 by The Summary channel).

## Relevance to the source framework

Schwager's interview-based methodology surfaces patterns across dozens of successful traders. Key themes from the Market Wizards series that appear in the source framework:

### Variant perception
The concept of holding a well-researched view that differs from market consensus — and having the conviction to act on it — is a recurring theme across Schwager's interviews with hedge fund managers. See [[variant-perception]] ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

### Risk discipline
Virtually every successful trader Schwager interviewed emphasized pre-defined risk limits, mechanical loss-cutting, and the separation of ego from trading decisions. This directly informs [[risk-management]] and the framing of [[behavioral-traps]].

### Process over outcomes
Schwager's subjects consistently distinguish between the quality of a decision and the outcome of a trade — a distinction that maps directly to [[behavioral-traps#3. Confusing outcomes with decisions]].
