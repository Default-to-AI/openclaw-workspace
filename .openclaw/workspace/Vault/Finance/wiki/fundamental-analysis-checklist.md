# Fundamental Analysis Checklist

A gate-based checklist applied to every candidate that survives [[idea-sourcing|initial screening]]. The purpose is to disqualify fast and allocate analytical time only to ideas that clear all four gates.

## Gate 1 — Circle of competence

> Never invest in a business you do not fundamentally understand.

If you cannot clearly articulate how the company makes money, what drives its unit economics, and how it scales, the idea is discarded immediately. This is not about industry expertise — it is about being honest with yourself regarding what you can and cannot evaluate. The concept is most strongly associated with [[warren-buffett|Warren Buffett]] and Charlie Munger ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

**Practical test**: Can you explain the business model to someone with no financial background in under two minutes? If not, you do not understand it well enough to bet capital on it.

## Gate 2 — Debt and financial health

Prioritize companies with:
- **Zero long-term debt**, or
- **Steadily declining** long-term debt over multiple years

A pristine balance sheet ensures survival during credit freezes, recessions, and black-swan events. Companies that rely on continuous refinancing are structurally fragile — they can be fundamentally sound and still go bankrupt if credit markets seize.

**What to check**: Long-term debt on the balance sheet across 5+ years of annual reports. Trend matters more than a single snapshot.

## Gate 3 — Management alignment

Signals of aligned management:
- **High insider ownership** — executives and directors with meaningful personal stakes
- **Open-market purchases** — insiders buying shares with their own money (not exercising options)
- **Reasonable compensation** — pay packages that are proportional to company size and performance

Red flags on the management side:
- Excessive executive compensation relative to peers and performance
- History of insider selling, especially at elevated prices
- Compensation structures that reward short-term stock price over long-term value creation

Insider buying is one of the strongest asymmetric signals available: insiders sell for many reasons (diversification, taxes, personal liquidity), but they buy for only one reason — they believe the stock is cheap ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

## Gate 4 — Red flags (immediate disqualifiers)

| Red Flag | Why It Kills |
|----------|-------------|
| Inventories growing significantly faster than sales | Signals demand deterioration or channel stuffing; future write-downs likely |
| Reckless acquisition spree | Empire-building management destroying shareholder value; integration risk compounds |
| Share consolidation (reverse splits) | Almost always a cosmetic fix for a structurally declining stock price |
| Dividend yield > 7% | Market is pricing in a dividend cut; the yield is an illusion |

Any single red flag is sufficient to discard the idea. These patterns are empirically associated with capital destruction ([[(To Ingest) YouTube Research - Stock Picking Hedge Fund Strategy|source]]).

## Integration with workflow

In the [[hedge-fund-strategy-research#Weekly workflow|weekly workflow]], this checklist is executed during the Sunday deep-dive session. One to two candidates from the quantitative screen receive full checklist treatment: annual report review, debt trend analysis, insider transaction review, and compensation evaluation.
