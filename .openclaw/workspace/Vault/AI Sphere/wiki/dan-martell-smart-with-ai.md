# Source Summary: Dangerously Smart With AI (Dan Martell)

A framework for shifting from an AI "doer" (using AI as a calculator to do tasks faster) to an AI "director" (using AI to upgrade learning, inputs, and decision-making).

## Core Concepts

### 1. The Calculator Trap
Most people use AI to save time on tasks (emails, summaries). High-leverage users use AI to improve their thinking and cognitive capabilities.

### 2. Upgrading Inputs
- The output of the brain depends on the input data.
- **Actionable tactics:**
  1. Reset social media algorithms to only feed high-signal educational content.
  2. Setup an automated daily AI briefing on critical industry news (summarize top 3 items + "why it matters").
  3. Use **NotebookLM** for "Just In Time" learning rather than "Just In Case" consumption (create podcast/quizzes dynamically before making a decision).

### 3. Upgrading Outputs via [[ai-red-teaming]]
- The biggest threat to business is falling in love with your own untested ideas.
- Use AI as a ruthless red team partner because it has no ego and infinite knowledge.
- **Workflow:**
  1. Find the fatal flaw (Pre-mortem from 6 months in the future).
  2. Eliminate blind spots (Prompt as a cynical competitor with constraints).
  3. Rank risks and build contingency plans for each.

### 4. The 92/8 Rule & Identity Shift
- AI can handle 92% of tasks (writing, analysis, research).
- **The 8% humans must focus on:**
  - **Taste:** Knowing what looks great.
  - **Vision:** Understanding what the future should look like.
  - **Care:** Emotional connection and enrolling people.
- **Action:** audit your weekly calendar. Find tasks that are "hard for computers, easy for humans" (detecting sarcasm, reading tone, ethical calls). Automate the rest. Mentioned tools include **Manis AI** and **[[openclaw|OpenClaw]]** for advanced automation.
